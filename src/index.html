Kyoto

Kyoto, a city where tradition meets modernity, is renowned for its historic temples, shrines, and preserved districts such as Gion. This former capital of Japan is enveloped by mountains, offering a picturesque backdrop to its rich cultural tapestry. Known for landmarks like the Golden Pavilion and Fushimi Inari Shrine, Kyoto is a hub of traditional practices and crafts.

Top Attractions

  1. Kinkakuji Temple

    Kinkakuji Temple, or the Golden Pavilion, stands out in Kyoto, Japan, for its stunning golden exterior that reflects beautifully in the surrounding pond, creating a mesmerizing sight. Click here for more info.

  2. Nijo Castle

    Nijo Castle, nestled in Kyoto, Japan, stands as a majestic representation of the country's feudal history, originally serving as the residence for Tokugawa Ieyasu, the first shogun of the Edo Period. Click here for more info.

  3. Arashiyama

    Arashiyama, situated on the western outskirts of Kyoto, Japan, is a scenic area known for its natural beauty and historic sites. It is famed for the Arashiyama Bamboo Grove, a breathtaking pathway surrounded by towering bamboo, offering a serene and otherworldly experience. Click here for more info.

  4. Gion

    The most famous neighborhood in Kyoto is also known as the Geisha district. It is here, among the tea houses and private clubs, that you can catch a glimpse of a geisha in full dress including white makeup. Click here for more info.

  5. Ginkakuji Temple

    Built in 1482 by the shogun Ashikaga Yoshimasa, this Zen temple was supposed to have been finished in a coat of silver. Although this step never occurred, the temple is still known by the name Silver Pavilion. Click here for more info.

Travel Tips

  • Explore Beyond the Beaten Path.
  • Don’t miss out on Kyoto’s culinary delights.
  • Kyoto's beauty peaks during cherry blossom season.
  • Respect local customs and manners.
  • Many places in Kyoto, including temples still prefer cash.
